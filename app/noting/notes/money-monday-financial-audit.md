# The Roth IRA Back Door You're Sleeping On

Tax season reminder most people miss:

You can still contribute to your **2025 Roth IRA** until **April 15, 2026**. That means right now — today — you can open a Roth, put in up to $7,000 ($8,000 if you're 50+), and it counts as last year's contribution.

**You can withdraw your principal anytime.** No penalties. No waiting period. Put in $3,500 today, need it back in 6 months — take it out. Zero fines.

If you *don't* need it? It just sits there compounding tax-free. For decades.

The only risk is not doing it. April 15 is the hard cutoff.

## Want to Go Deeper? Run a Full Financial Audit

The Roth IRA move is one thing. But if you want the full picture — where your money's actually going, what you're overpaying for, what you're missing — you have two paths.

**What I do:** I use [Monarch Money](https://www.monarchmoney.com/) to track everything automatically. It pulls from all my accounts, categorizes spending, and I have my AI agent run audits on it regularly. I don't have to think about it — the data's always there.

**If you don't want to pay for a service:** You can do the same thing manually with a bank statement export and Claude. It takes about 15 minutes and surfaces the same insights. You just have to do it yourself each time.

Either way works. One costs money and saves time. The other costs time and saves money. Pick whichever fits.

---

### Step 1: Export Your Bank Statement

Log into your bank's website or app. Look for **"Download Statements"** or **"Export Transactions."**

- **Chase:** Statements & Documents → Download (PDF or CSV)
- **Bank of America:** Statements & Documents → Download
- **Wells Fargo:** Statements & Documents → Select date range → Download
- **Capital One:** Statements → Download CSV
- **Any bank:** Search "export" or "download" in your app. Most support CSV or PDF.

**Pick the last 90 days.** That's enough to see patterns without drowning in data.

If your bank gives you options: **CSV is best** (Claude reads it cleanly). PDF works too.

---

### Step 2: Open Claude and Paste This Prompt

Go to [claude.ai](https://claude.ai) and start a new conversation. Upload your bank statement file, then paste this:

```
You are my personal financial analyst. I just uploaded my bank/credit card data. Please run a complete audit:

1. SPENDING ANALYSIS
   - Top 10 spending categories (last 90 days)
   - Month-over-month trend — am I spending more or less?
   - Subscriptions I might be forgetting about
   - Any unusual or one-time charges worth flagging

2. SAVINGS & INVESTMENT CHECK
   - Am I maxing tax-advantaged accounts? (401k, Roth IRA, HSA)
   - Cash sitting idle that could be working harder?

3. TAX SEASON QUICK WINS (if before April 15)
   - Can I still contribute to last year's Roth IRA?
   - Any deductions I might be missing?

4. TOP 3 ACTIONS I SHOULD TAKE THIS WEEK
   - Ranked by impact. Be specific. No vague advice.

Be direct. If something looks bad, say so. No sugarcoating.
```

That's it. Claude will read your statement and give you a full breakdown.

---

### Step 3: Do the One Thing It Tells You

You don't need to fix everything today. Just do the #1 action it suggests. Usually takes less than 20 minutes.

Then just do it. One action. Done.
